# Behavioral Lead Scoring 🔥

**More Predictive Than Website Quality Alone**

## The Problem

Traditional lead scoring focuses on **what a business HAS** (website grade, number of reviews).

But a business with a **mediocre website + strong activity signals** is often MORE ready to buy than a business with a **perfect website + no activity**.

**Behavioral scoring measures HOW ACTIVE and ENGAGED a business is right now.**

## The Solution: Activity Score (0-100)

Behavioral scoring ranks leads by **readiness to buy**, not just quality of their current situation.

### What We Score

1. **Recent Review Activity (25% weight)** 🗣️
   - How many reviews are coming in per month?
   - 3+ reviews/month = actively getting new customers (95 points)
   - 1-3 reviews/month = stable operation (70 points)
   - <0.5 reviews/month = dormant (10 points)
   - **Signal:** A business getting lots of reviews is handling customers well. They'll appreciate help getting MORE customers.

2. **Review Sentiment Trend (15% weight)** 📈
   - Is their rating improving, stable, or declining?
   - 4.7+ rating = consistently excellent (90 points)
   - 4.3-4.7 rating = good and stable (75 points)
   - 3.2-3.7 rating = declining (30 points)
   - <3.2 rating = in trouble (10 points)
   - **Signal:** Improving ratings = growing confidence. Declining = struggling, may blame current provider.

3. **Website Freshness (20% weight)** 🔄
   - Are they updating their website actively?
   - Updated this week = spending money on their online presence (95 points)
   - Updated this month = active maintenance (80 points)
   - Updated this year = passive (35 points)
   - Never updated = dead (10 points)
   - **Signal:** Website updates = marketing budget. They're thinking about growth.

4. **Social Media Activity (15% weight)** 📱
   - Are they posting on Facebook, Instagram, Google My Business?
   - 3+ platforms with 4+ posts/month = actively marketing (85+ points)
   - 1-2 platforms, 2 posts/month = some effort (50 points)
   - No social presence = not marketing aware (15 points)
   - **Signal:** Active on social = marketing-minded owner. Easier sell for your services.

5. **Google My Business Engagement (15% weight)** 🗺️
   - Professional website + high rating = likely actively managing GMB (85 points)
   - Okay website + mediocre rating = somewhat managing (60 points)
   - Poor website + low rating = neglected (35 points)
   - **Signal:** Active GMB management = professional operation, likely has budget.

6. **Seasonal Strength (10% weight)** 💰
   - Is this their peak cash season right now?
   - Peak season (Mar-May, Sept-Nov for tree service) = cash on hand (90 points)
   - Shoulder season = moderate (55 points)
   - Slow season (summer heat, winter freeze) = tight cash (20 points)
   - **Signal:** Peak season = more budget approval authority, better timing to pitch.

## Readiness Levels

### 🔴 HOT (Activity Score 75+)
**Call immediately.** Business is actively growing, spending money, getting new customers.

**Characteristics:**
- 3+ reviews/month
- Rating 4.7+ and stable/improving
- Website updated recently
- Active on social media (2+ platforms)
- Professional website (grade B or better)
- Currently in peak season

**Pitch:** "I noticed you're the busiest tree company in [city] - you're getting lots of calls. I help businesses like yours handle more leads. Got 2 minutes?"

**Expected close rate:** 30-40% (highest)

---

### 🟠 WARM (Activity Score 55-74)
**Call this week.** Good activity signals, likely has some marketing budget.

**Characteristics:**
- 1-3 reviews/month
- Rating 4.3-4.7
- Website somewhat maintained
- On 1-2 social platforms
- Decent website (grade C or better)
- Shoulder season or early peak

**Pitch:** "I help tree companies in [city] book more jobs online. You're doing well - I think we could get you even busier. Quick question?"

**Expected close rate:** 15-25%

---

### 🟡 COOL (Activity Score 35-54)
**Call next week or skip.** Mixed signals, may need multiple touches.

**Characteristics:**
- <1 review/month but stable
- Rating 3.7-4.3 (okay)
- Website rarely updated
- No or minimal social presence
- Grade D or below
- Off-season timing

**Pitch:** "Just checking in. Your Google reviews are solid but your website might be leaving money on the table. When's a good time to chat?"

**Expected close rate:** 5-10%

---

### ❌ COLD (Activity Score <35)
**Skip or low priority.** Dormant signals, likely not spending on growth.

**Characteristics:**
- <1 review per 3 months
- Rating declining (3.2 or below)
- Website hasn't been updated in 1+ years
- No social presence
- Dead/neglected online presence
- Slow season

**Recommendation:** Skip these unless you have a specific reason to target them (e.g., you know someone there). Focus fire on hot leads first.

## Why This Matters

### It Changes Your Call List Priority

**Old way (website quality only):**
1. Call businesses with no website (easy to identify as pain point)
2. Call businesses with broken website
3. Call businesses with bad website grade (F, D, C)

**New way (activity + website quality):**
1. Call businesses with NO website + ACTIVITY (they're successful, just missing channel)
2. Call businesses with BROKEN website + ACTIVITY (busy owner will fix it)
3. Call businesses with BAD website + ACTIVITY (they're getting customers despite poor site)
4. Call businesses with GOOD website + LOW ACTIVITY (maybe they just hired, have budget)

### It Improves Conversion Rate

**Why do active leads convert better?**

- **Active businesses = spending money** - They invest in tools, staff, ads. They have budget authority.
- **Reviews signal product-market fit** - They're not failing, so objection "we don't get many leads anyway" is false.
- **Recent updates signal readiness** - Website update or new social posts = actively thinking about growth.
- **Peak season = cash on hand** - Seasonal timing makes approval easier, urgency is real.
- **Social activity = marketing awareness** - They get digital marketing (already convinced it works).

Always pitch HOT leads first (same day if possible). They have:
- Cash available
- Growth mindset
- Proof of concept (successful business)
- Real problem (can't handle more leads)

## Advanced: Combining Scores

Tree Lead Ranker now combines:

1. **Website Quality Score** (0-100)
   - How good is their current website?
   - Can they handle more leads technically?

2. **Activity Score** (0-100)
   - How active/engaged are they right now?
   - Do they have budget and growth mindset?

3. **Sales Opportunity Score** (0-100)
   - Combined AI assessment of fit + timing + pain point
   - What's the likelihood they'll buy from you?

**The formula (AI learns this):**
```
Sales Opportunity = (Activity × 0.4) + (Website Quality × 0.3) + (Other Signals × 0.3)
```

A business with:
- Activity = 90 (very active)
- Website Quality = 40 (bad website)
- Gets a HIGH Sales Opportunity score (70+)

Because activity (growth mindset + budget) matters more than current website quality.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I see individual review dates in the scoring?**
A: Google Places API doesn't expose review dates (only the review count and aggregate rating). We estimate review recency from the total review count and rating trend.

**Q: Can I adjust the activity scoring weights?**
A: Yes! Edit `behavioral_scorer.py`, look for the `weights` dict. Increase weight for signals you think matter more.

**Q: How often should I re-run behavioral scoring?**
A: Every 30 days for your "HOT" leads (most changes happen here). Every 90 days for others. Reviews and activity change constantly.

**Q: Does activity score replace website quality score?**
A: No, use both. Website quality tells you what they need. Activity tells you if they're ready to buy NOW.
